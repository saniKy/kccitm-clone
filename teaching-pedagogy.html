Teaching Pedagogy

The institute's main goal is to help students discover their potential as professionals by developing their untapped abilities and talents. To bring the best out of a student, it calls for a strategically designed courseware & teaching methodology. A bright future for kids cannot be guaranteed by simply imparting knowledge from teacher to students. For the students to have a bright future, it is crucial that they apply and remember that knowledge. No effort is spared to make sure that the teaching methodology is such that guarantees the delivery of best academic and practical knowledge to the student.

The journey of a student at KCC, from an aspiring professional to a real one gets concluded after he goes through a joyful academic journey wherein each of the following teaching methodology contributes to his success.

Case Study Learning

Case study learning proves to be a very productive platform for the students to work upon their ability to analyze problems and thereon developing a rational approach to problem solving techniques.

Initially adopted by business schools in the US, case study learning has now become an integral element of teaching and learning pedagogy in all the major fields of study at top institutes in India.

In this method, students are asked to analyze a specific situation or challenge by business and industry called a "case".

At the KCC Institute of Technology and Management, the case study method is used extensively at both the management and engineering schools to expose students to strategies deployed to arrive at solutions using a problem-solving approach. Students are asked to make live presentations using the case study method which is followed by an in-depth discussion of the case.

>>

Intellectual Sports

The students are indulged in such games and activities which in turn harness their decision making skills, presentation skills and innovative skills.

The game sessions also make the student more pro-active and an indulgent team player. Intellectual sports such as role playing, creative mind games and extempore presentations help students to further refine their analytical, decision-making, team building and participation skills.

They also help with building self-confidence and self-esteem. AT the KCC Institute of Technology and Management, these activities are conducted regularly.

While many events are routinely organized by departments as a part of instruction delivery, students also organize these events during informal group meetings, competitions and the annual KCC Student Festival. Students take part in these events with great enthusiasm and gain a lot from them in a variety of ways.

>>

Group Discussions

Healthy group discussions are promoted so that the students become more expressive, and well-articulated and at the same time inculcate the spirit of participation in them.

At KCC, student involvement in group discussions (GDs) is not restricted to the placement season.

They are frequently conducted in small groups under the guidance of faculty members to teach students how to communicate a perspective or a point of view in front of an informed audience.

This way, any learning gaps are identified well ahead of the placement season when companies formally arrive on campus to recruit students for full-time positions. Many of the leading organizations that visit the KCC campus each year conduct group discussions as a part of their student evaluation and assessment endeavors.

>>

Industry Visits

During the course, students have the opportunity to tour a variety of corporate and industrial buildings, to gain a sense of the working conditions there.

The purpose of the visits is to help the students make connections between academics and real-world situations. The KCC student mandate includes practical and hands-on exposure to business and industry.

Throughout the academic period, both the undergraduate and postgraduate students at the KCC Institute of Technology and Management, frequently take part in industry visits. Department heads carefully select these locations, which are typically well-suited for day visits, to give the endeavor purpose and substance.

While engineering students tour manufacturing and research facilities, management students explore prominent multinational corporations to gain practical insights into the operations of big enterprises. When students graduate and are prepared to enter the ranks of their various professions, industry visits open doors for internships and potential jobs.

>>

Classroom Learning

Students can absorb the knowledge imparted to them by theoretical lectures given by eminent faculty members in a very laid-back setting during classroom instruction. Classroom learning also gives them the change to participate in question and answer sessions.

Presentations

At periodic intervals, the students are encouraged to undertake project work and give presentation on their chosen subject under the guidance of their guide faculty. By the end of the course this enriching exercises undertaken by the students hone their presentation skills.

Role Playing

Under this medium of instruction the students play designated roles relevant to real-life situations. This activity allows the students experience the dynamics of real life management situations.

Brain Storming Sessions

These sessions greatly helpful in the student’s development of their problem-solving skills. The goal of these sessions is to reduce the group social inhibitions, which will encourage the development of fresh concepts, solutions, and viewpoints.
