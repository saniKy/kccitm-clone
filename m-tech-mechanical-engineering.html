M.Tech in Mechanical Engineering

Course Overview

Apply Online

Mechanical engineering is regarded as one of the most dynamic disciplines within the field of engineering. It applies the theories, principles and concepts of engineering to the design and manufacturing of many of the systems and products we use daily. The discipline leverages key technologies such as CAD/CAM, thermodynamics, fluid mechanics and product lifecycle management to enhance the quality of our lives through innovation. Mechanical engineers construct manufacturing facilities, plants, transportation systems, industrial equipment and a dazzling array of machinery to build a better planet and sustain it.

The M.Tech. degree in Mechanical Engineering at the KCC Institute of Technology and Management is a specialized post graduate qualification that is designed to enable students to assume leadership roles in an industrial setting by demonstrating superior levels of competency and performance not only on a production floor but also in a design lab. The degree is of two years duration full-time. It is recognized by UGC and AICTE. The degree is awarded by Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University.
Eligibility
Candidates should have a Bachelor’s degree from a recognized university in the stream in which they wish to pursue an M.Tech. at KCC. Candidates must have passed B.E/B.Tech. in their respective disciplines with a minimum of 60% in aggregate. SC/ST/PwD candidates.

Can apply with a minimum of 54% marks. Non GATE candidates are granted admission to M.Tech. degrees on the basis of their Qualifying Exam Marks for the vacant seats left after GATE candidates are allotted seats.
Procedure
Admission is granted through competitive performance in the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE). Reservation in admission to the M.Tech. degrees are governed by AICTE/State Government/ Central Government rules.

Management seats are filled directly by the institute by conducting institute level counseling based on the merit of SEE- UPTU : 2026 / AIEEE rank & merit in qualifying examination.

Higher Studies Options

Students who complete the M.Tech. degree in Mechanical Engineering are eligible to pursue a Ph.D. in a variety of super specializations within the field of mechanical engineering. They include but are not limited to the following:

  • Construction
  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Heat, Power & Thermal Engineering
  • Hydraulics Engineering
  • Material Science & Technology
  • Nano Technology
  • Thermodynamics
  • Transportation
