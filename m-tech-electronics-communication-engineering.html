M.Tech Electronics & Communication

Course Overview

Apply Online

In an all-encompassing world of advanced technologies in which machines talk to other machines as well as human beings, the ability to design, develop and implement underlying infrastructures to support such communication is key to sustained growth and progress. The seamless convergence of a full spectrum of diverse technologies has created intense demand for engineers who not only understand these technologies but can also effortlessly articulate both the ways and means in which to interact digitally across industries, protocols and even geographical boundaries.

Electronics and communication engineers architect the underlying edifice of the Internet to send and receive billions of data packets every second around the world and even into space. At a fundamental level, electronics and communication engineers adopt active go-to-market strategies to design electronic circuits, devices, microprocessors, microcontrollers and systems to manage, monitor and optimize the performance of electronic devices that enhance the quality of our lives in one way or another. From radars to sonars and from mobiles to motherboards, electronics and communication engineering has touched literally every facet of our existence.

The M.Tech. degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering at the KCC Institute of Technology and Management is an industry-focused post graduate course that aims to further refine the analytical, research and cognitive capabilities of students who have already achieved a thorough foundation in electronics, communication, telecommunication or a related field by equipping them with an enhanced skills set that serves as viable pathways to industrial settings globally. The degree is of two years duration and is pursued by students at KCC as a full-time course. It is approved by AICTE. The degree is awarded by Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Technical University formally known as Uttar Pradesh Technical University (UPTU)
Eligibility
Candidates should have a Bachelor’s degree from a recognized university in the stream in which they wish to pursue an M.Tech. at KCC. Candidates must have passed B.E/B.Tech. in their respective disciplines with a minimum of 60% in aggregate. SC/ST/PwD candidates.

Can apply with a minimum of 54% marks. Non GATE candidates are granted admission to M.Tech. degrees on the basis of their Qualifying Exam Marks for the vacant seats left after GATE candidates are allotted seats.
Procedure
Admission is granted through competitive performance in the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE). Reservation in admission to the M.Tech. degrees are governed by AICTE/State Government/ Central Government rules.

Management seats are filled directly by the institute by conducting institute level counseling based on the merit of SEE- AKTU : 2026 / AIEEE rank & merit in qualifying examination.

Higher Studies Options

Upon completing the M.Tech. degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from KCC, students can choose one of several different pathways with a view to pursuing higher education. Students can receive additional training with a super specialized focus by joining a research lab either in government or private industry. They can also enroll in a doctoral degree program either in India or abroad. Some of the areas in which students can obtain a Ph.D. degree based on their under graduate and post graduate education in the field include the following:

  • Communication Systems
  • Computer Networks
  • Digital Systems
  • Mobile Technology
  • Nano Engineering
  • VSLI Systems
